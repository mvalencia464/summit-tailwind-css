Who can benefit?

Medical Centers

In a medical center, it is crucial to have all staff trained in BLS/First Aid/CPR to provide quick and effective care to patients.

Center staff can respond to emergencies such as cardiac arrest, stroke, or respiratory distress, and provide lifesaving treatment until emergency medical services arrive.

Elderly Care

In a nursing home, assisted living, or home care setting, it is essential to have staff trained in BLS/First Aid/CPR to provide quick and effective care to residents.

With proper training, staff can respond to emergencies such as chest pain, stroke, or sudden cardiac arrest and provide lifesaving treatment until emergency medical services arrive.

Child Care Facilities

In a childcare setting, it is important to have staff trained in BLS/First Aid/CPR to provide quick and effective care to children.

With this training, childcare staff can respond to emergencies such as choking, breathing emergencies, or sudden cardiac arrest, and provide lifesaving treatment until emergency medical services arrive.

Babysitting

Babysitters are often the first line of defense in case of an emergency with a child.

The Babysitting course training can help them respond effectively in case of a choking or breathing emergency, or if the child is unconscious. They will also learn how to use CPR/First Aid which can be lifesaving in case of a cardiac emergency.

Wilderness First Aid

In remote outdoor settings, quick and effective first aid can mean the difference between life and death.

The First Aid training can help wilderness guides, hikers, and campers respond effectively to emergencies such as heat stroke, hypothermia, broken bones, scrapes and bad cuts. They will also learn how to provide First Aid/CPR for more severe emergencies such as heart attack or drowning.

Commercial Industrial Job Sites

In a commercial or industrial setting, it is critical to have employees trained in BLS/First Aid/CPR to respond to workplace accidents or medical emergencies.

Employees can provide lifesaving treatment for emergencies such as electrical shock, chemical exposure, or respiratory distress. They will also learn how to use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED), which can be lifesaving in case of a cardiac emergency.

Group Family Sessions

Children ages 8 and up are encouraged to learn these skills as well.

You never know when an emergency will arise in your own home and being prepared in BLS/First Aid/CPR will mean everything!

Everyday General Emergencies

In everyday life, BLS/First Aid/CPR training can equip individuals with the skills and knowledge to react effectively to emergencies such as choking, chest pain, or sudden cardiac arrest.

Training can also provide peace of mind and increase personal safety, knowing that you have the skills and knowledge to respond in case of an emergency.
